"Afghan Red beans and Rice"

This is one of my favorite meals. It was the first meal I prepped with my significant other, and was created by merging the flavors of our childhood. He grew up eating a classic southern dish “Red beans and Rice,” and I grew up eating “Lubya” (aka: “Afghan Kidney bean Curry”). We both realized they have similar ingredients and thought it would be fun to merge the two recipes because that’s how most of the great recipes are created...right?!

Just like Red beans and Rice, there are many varieties of Lubya. Depending on who you ask, each recipe can have a drastically different taste from the last. Lubya is a dish that can be found in a number of the neighboring Central to South Asian countries, if you ever have the opportunity I suggest trying the other variations to experience the exquisite flavors that each country adds to their version of the dish.  


Unlike some of the other versions, my Afghan Lubya is simplistic in it’s ingredients while capturing an equivalent punch of flavor. The wonderful blend of coriander, cumin, and turmeric highlights the spice blend which we found plays well with the US southern staple. 

Ingredients
  1. 2 tbsp coconut oil - divided
  2. 1 tbsp whole coriander seed
  3. 1 tbsp whole cumin seed
  4. 1/2 tsp dried mint - (optional)
  5. 1 lb. andouille sausage 
  6. 1 Green Bell Pepper, chopped
  7. 3 ribs celery - chopped
  8. 1 1/2 cups whole tomatoes - (1 15 oz can)
  9. 1 small red onion - small dice, some reserved for garnish
  10. 4 cloves garlic - minced
  11. 2 tsp. cayenne pepper
  12. 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  13. 1 tsp. dried oregano
  14. 2 tbsp parsley
  15. 1 bay leaf
  16. 2 tsp. Hot sauce
  17. 1 cup vegetable broth
  18. 3 cups kidney beans - (2 15 oz cans), rinced and drained
  19. 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper - for taste
  20. 1 tsp kosher salt - for taste
  21. cilantro or sliced green onions - for garnish
  22. basmati rice - to serve
  1. In a large bowl, season the chicken breast with salt and pepper. Let it sit for 15 minutes to marinate. Brown the chicken, then remove from pot.
  2. Stir fry onions on coconut oil till they turn a golden/translucent color, add coriander and whole cumin seeds.   
  3. Add the 1 1/2 cups of whole tomatoes (into the pan with onions). Mash them up and bring them to a simmer.   
  4. Pour the kidney beans (from step 1) into a separate pan. Add water until the top of the kidney beans are covered. Add garlic, cayenne, parsley, bay leaves, thyme, celery, and green pepper. Stir a few times. If the mixture appears dry, add half a cup of water to the mix.   
  5. Add sausages and cook for 30 minutes.                                                                                              

  6. In a large saucepan, mix the chicken, and everything else made (step 3-6). Stir. Add 1 cup of vegetable chicken broth. Stir again. Simmer for another 15 minutes.   

  7. Separately, obtain a couple of scoops of rice to mix in the saucepan (your preference on how much rice you want to be mixed).
  8. Serve the chicken, rice, and everything in the saucepan on top of the white basmati rice.       
  9. Garnish with red onion, dried mint (optional) and cilantro. I recommend the dried mint because it is a common ingredient in Afghani dishes.
  10. Enjoy!
